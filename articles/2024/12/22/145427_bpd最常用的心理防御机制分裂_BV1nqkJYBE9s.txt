Here's a structured summary of the core ideas:

*   **Core Concept: Splitting (分裂)**
    *   A primitive defense mechanism where an individual perceives others as either completely good or completely bad, unable to integrate both aspects.
    *   Characterized by rapid shifts in perception of a person from one extreme to the other.
*   **Types and Manifestation:**
    *   Common in Borderline Personality Disorder (边缘形人格障碍) and Disorganized Attachment Style (混乱形依恋).
    *   In BPD, splitting extends to most relationships. In disorganized attachment, primarily affects intimate relationships.
    *   Individuals often use moralistic judgment to attack their partners, labeling them as entirely bad.
*   **Formation of Splitting:**
    *   Arises from inconsistent parenting where the same behavior is met with both praise and punishment (good and bad responses) at different times.
    *   The child fails to integrate the good and bad aspects of a parent into a cohesive image.
*   **Purpose of Splitting:**
    *   Avoids the painful internal conflict of not being able to integrate positive and negative perceptions.
    *   Offers a way to manage overwhelming and confusing emotions.
*   **Negative Consequences:**
    *   If used constantly in adult relationships, it is difficult for partners to cope with.

**Fundamental Point:** Splitting is a defense mechanism that results from an inability to integrate positive and negative aspects of others, leading to extreme and unstable perceptions.
