**What is Emotional Bullying?**

*   Emotional bullies are people who habitually complain, criticize, express negativity, or vent their frustrations onto others.
*   They use others as emotional dumping grounds, not considering the impact of their behavior.
*   These behaviors can manifest as incessant complaining, blaming, negativity, and even anger directed towards the recipient.
*   The constant negativity and venting can be draining and can lead the recipient to feel depressed, anxious, and emotionally exhausted.

**Examples of Emotional Bullying:**

*   A close friend constantly complains about their work, family, and life, ignoring the listener's feelings and needs.
*   A friend who faced a setback became relentlessly negative, and pulled down a positive friend, leading both to give up.
*   A friend who consistently dumps their problems and negative emotions at late hours, leaving the other feeling drained.

**The Impact of Emotional Bullying:**

*   Exposure to constant negativity can negatively impact the recipient's mood, causing them to become more anxious, stressed, and even depressed.
*   It can lead to a feeling of being drained, emotionally exhausted, and can impact their own personal life and productivity.
*   It can cause you to internalize others' negativity and impact your own well-being and outlook.
*   It can disrupt your emotional state, causing you to become irritable or experience other negative side effects, such as insomnia.

**How to Deal with Emotional Bullies:**

The article suggests adopting a set of principles:

1.  **Release Your Burden:**
    *   Don't assume that other people's bad moods are a result of your actions. Their emotions are often due to their own issues.
    *   Don't take responsibility for other people's negative feelings or try to fix them.
    *   Focus on taking care of your emotional well-being and not absorbing others' negativity.

2.  **Maintain Coldness & Do Not Respond:**
    *   Sometimes, it's best to stay neutral and avoid becoming emotionally involved in others' complaints.
    *   Don't allow their toxic emotions to overwhelm you; create a personal "emotional shield."
    *   Remember that it's not your job to fix their problems.

3.  **Be Courageous to Refuse and Resolutely Give Up:**
    *   It's okay to decline to engage in their emotional rants, and set boundaries.
    *   It's important to stop giving in to unreasonable emotional demands or outbursts of anger.
    *   Sometimes it's best to distance yourself from the person if the negative behavior continues despite setting boundaries.
    *   Don't feel bad about giving up relationships that consistently drain you and negatively impact you.
