**Summary:**

**I. Core Problems with Apologies:**
   *  **Faulty Apologies:**
        *  Shifting blame, not taking responsibility.
        *  Using emotional manipulation to force forgiveness.
        *  Lacking sincerity, omitting words of remorse like "I'm sorry."
   *  **Why these are difficult to deal with:**
        *  People often try to reason with the apologizer, triggering defensiveness.
        *  Defensive individuals with fragile egos often refuse to acknowledge fault, and their flawed apologies can be worse.
        *  Focusing on forcing a "good" apology often reinforces their unwillingness to apologize properly.

**II. Effective Strategies for Dealing with Difficult Apologizers**

*   **Accept that a perfect apology might not happen**: Forcing or waiting for sincere apologies is not realistic with difficult people, don't set this as the requirement for your healing.
*   **Adjust Expectations of Forgiveness**:
    *  Don't feel obligated to forgive; you have the right to choose the level of forgiveness or not forgive at all.
    *  The feeling of being pressured to forgive fuels anger.
    *  The goal of healing is to move past anger, not to force forgiveness.

*   **Validating your feelings:**
        * Understanding your experience and not being forced to forgive reduces anger and resentment.
    * **Personal Choices on Forgiveness:**
       *    You can choose the level of forgiveness based on the situation, depth of hurt, and time passed.
            *  **Example 1:** Choosing not to forgive if the hurt is deep and the person is unremorseful.
            *  **Example 2:** Partial forgiveness if the relationship is valuable and you understand the apologizer.
            * **Example 3:**  Gradual forgiveness (from 80% to 95% ) if some time passed, and you see efforts.

*   **Setting Boundaries:**
      * Establishing healthy boundaries is essential for your peace of mind and healing.
*   **Communication:**
    * Direct communication with the apologizer about how you feel can facilitate the process of healing.
*   **Self-agency in forgiveness:**
        * You are in control of how, when and if you forgive, not others.

**III. Conclusion:**
 * **Core Point:** Forgiveness is a personal choice and is not required for healing; the key is to reduce personal anger and resentment.
 *  **Fundamental Point:** It is healthier to focus on personal boundaries and emotional well-being rather than relying on a perfect apology from difficult people.

**IV. Overarching Framework:**

This content discusses problematic apologies, explaining why they fail, and proposes a solution of focusing on one’s healing rather than the act of receiving a ‘perfect’ apology.  It gives a framework for people who have been on the receiving end of bad apologies, to move forward from their hurt and make a personal decision on forgiveness.
