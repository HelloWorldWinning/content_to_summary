**Summary:**

**I. Stages of Leaving an NVD (Narcissistic Victim Disabler):**

*   **A. Confusion Stage:**
    *   Characterized by emotional turmoil and self-doubt due to NVD's manipulative tactics (love bombing, devaluation, discard, hoover).
    *   Victims experience emotional instability and struggle to understand their situation, often leading to self-blame.
*   **B. Awakening Stage:**
    *   The survivor begins to recognize that the NVD is intentionally manipulating their emotions.
    *   They start to seek external support and validate their feelings by sharing experiences with others.
*   **C. Fluctuating Stage:**
    *   Recognizes the NVD's true nature, but faces internal conflict and struggles to detach.
    *   Victims may experience repeated cycles of disengagement and being drawn back in by the NVD, causing significant disruption to their life.
*   **D. Calm Stage:**
    *   Clear understanding of the NVD's manipulative patterns and one's own vulnerabilities.
    *   This stage often includes an attempt to contact the survivor by the NVD, but the survivor recognizes it for the manipulation it is.
*   **E. Rebirth Stage:**
    *   The victim's self-worth is restored and their resilience significantly increases.
    *   Individuals focus on rebuilding their lives, healing from trauma, and establishing healthy boundaries.

**Core Point:**

Leaving an NVD relationship is not simply a breakup, but a profound psychological trauma requiring a complex healing process.

**Fundamental Point:**

The healing journey from an NVD relationship involves distinct stages, moving from confusion and self-blame to empowerment and self-reconstruction.

**Overarching Framework:**

The content outlines a five-stage model for understanding and recovering from a relationship with a Narcissistic Victim Disabler (NVD), emphasizing the psychological impact and the gradual process of healing and empowerment.
