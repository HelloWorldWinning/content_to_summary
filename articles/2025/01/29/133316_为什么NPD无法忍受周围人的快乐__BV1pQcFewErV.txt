**Summary:**

**I. Core Issues of Narcissistic Personality (NB):**

*   **Fragile Self-Esteem:** NB individuals have very fragile self-esteem despite their outward confidence.
*   **External Validation Dependency:** Their self-esteem is built on external praise and attention.
*   **Sensitivity to Others' Success:** They experience pain, unease, and jealousy when witnessing others' happiness or success.

**II. Reasons for NB's Intolerance of Others' Happiness:**

*   **A. Feeling Ignored:**
    *   They feel neglected when others receive attention and recognition.
    *   They are highly influenced by others' opinions.
    *   Comparisons lead to feelings of inadequacy.
*   **B. Fear of Losing Attention:**
    *   Others' happiness and success are perceived as threats.
    *   They fear being abandoned and losing others' interest and dependency.
*   **C. Constant Comparison:**
    *   They constantly compare themselves to others, affecting their self-worth.
    *   This comparison intensifies their anxiety, insecurity, and jealousy.
*   **D. Lack of Empathy:**
    *   They struggle to feel normal human emotions.
    *   They are preoccupied with whether they are being ignored or centered.
    *   This immature mindset creates envy and anxiety when others are happy.

**III. Reaction to Others' Happiness:**
*    They resort to demeaning others to protect their fragile self-esteem.

**Core Point:** NB individuals' intolerance of others' happiness stems from their fragile self-esteem, dependence on external validation, fear of losing attention, and lack of empathy, leading to constant comparisons and a need to demean others to feel better about themselves.

**Fundamental Point:** The core of NB’s struggle is rooted in an insecure sense of self that is excessively reliant on external validation and a deeply ingrained fear of losing attention and perceived importance.

**Overarching Framework:** The content presents a psychological analysis of Narcissistic Personality (NB), explaining why they have difficulty with others' happiness. This framework explores the roots of the issue in their fragile self-esteem and dependence on external validation and demonstrates how this insecurity affects their perception of and reaction to others' success.
