**Summary Outline:**

1.  **Reasons for NBD (Narcissistic Behavioral Disorder) Disengagement:**
    *   **Higher Value Target:** NBD prioritizes pursuing higher-value individuals, becoming too busy to return.
    *   **Ego Shattering:** NBD avoids reconnection after experiencing a severe narcissistic injury (e.g., public humiliation), often believing they are punishing you.
    *   **Avoidant/Nurse Type NBD:** NBD prioritizes interests over relationships, making the partner feel invisible, and may only provoke on social media, not engaging directly.
    *   **Perceived Strength/Awakening:** NBD avoids individuals who demonstrate strength and an inability to be manipulated, as they are no longer a valuable target.
2.  **Understanding "Hoovering" (回吸):**
    *   **Misplaced Value System:** The core reason for being drawn back (回吸) is a lost sense of personal value or low self-worth.
    *   **Objectification:** The NBD perceives the partner not as a human but as an object, connecting the partner's value to the NBD.
    *   **Hoovering is Not an Indicator of True Value:** Your value is independent of whether or not an NBD seeks to engage with you again.
3.  **Other relevant concepts:**
    *    Stockholm Syndrome and Trauma bonding as a reason for why a victim can be drawn back.

**Core Point:**

NBDs will completely disengage when they deem you no longer valuable as a source of narcissistic supply, indicating that reengagement is based on their needs, not your worth.

**Fundamental Point:**

Your personal value should not be defined by the actions of an NBD, and their attempts to "hoover" are not reflections of your true worth.

**Overarching Framework:**

The content focuses on understanding the behaviors of individuals with Narcissistic Behavioral Disorder (NBD), particularly why they sometimes disengage and why victims might desire reconnection ("hoovering"), emphasizing that an NBD's actions do not dictate the victim's worth or value. It also touched upon Stockholm syndrome and Trauma bonding in the context of how they might affect the victim's thinking.
