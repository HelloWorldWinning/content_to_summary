### Summary:

**I. Core Concepts:**

   *   **Ubiquity of "Small People" (小人):** In the workplace, the existence of "small people" (individuals who engage in underhanded tactics and backstabbing) is inevitable due to the power dynamics that create the need for such individuals to carry out negative tasks.
   *   **The "Big Person" (大人) Behind the "Small Person":**  "Small people" often act on behalf of a "big person" (a superior) who uses them to accomplish undesirable tasks while maintaining a clean public image.
   *   **Power Dynamics:** Power structures and hierarchical organizations fuel the presence of "small people," as they fulfill a need for those in power to have tasks done that they cannot do themselves or do not want to be associated with directly.
   *   **Three Functions of "Small People":** 1) Create conflict to maintain power balance, 2) engage in corruption without the direct responsibility of their superior, 3) do the "dirty work" while the leaders maintain a clean image.
   *   **Two Strategies for Dealing with "Small People":** "Treat the symptoms" and "Treat the root cause."
      *   **Treat the symptoms (治标):** Gather evidence of their wrongdoings, expose them publicly, and set boundaries to deter them from future attacks. This is a temporary solution, however.
      *   **Treat the root cause (治本):** Build relationships with superiors, create a network of support among colleagues, and gradually undermine their influence. This addresses the larger power dynamics that permit their behavior.

**II. Strategic Steps for Handling "Small People":**

   *   **Initial Evasion and Evidence Collection (逃避 + 收集证据):**  Don't engage in direct conflict initially. Instead, document their misdeeds with recordings, screenshots, and notes, including instances of gossip, sabotage and shirking duties.
   *   **"Cure the Symptoms" - Public Exposure and Confrontation (治标):**
        *   Present the collected evidence in a public setting to expose their negative behavior and create isolation and ostracization among their colleagues.
        *   Alternatively, confront them privately with the evidence to make them aware that their behaviour is known, without making yourself the enemy of the rest of the colleagues, that will prevent the small person from further badmouthing you.
   *  **"Cure the root cause" - Cultivate Relationships (治本):**
        *   **"Dig the foundations" (挖墙角):** Strengthen your relationship with your direct superior through consistent communication and active involvement in their priorities to create a buffer to the backstabbing done by "small people."
        *   **"Penetrate the core" (穿杀子):** Proactively engage with colleagues, build rapport, and establish yourself as a trustworthy team member to counter "small people"'s efforts to isolate you.
        *   **"Throw the stone" (甩石头):** Once you have the support of superiors and colleagues, strategically expose their negative actions, which ultimately will turn the tables against them.

**III. Mindset and Philosophy:**

   *  **Accept the Reality (接受现实):** Acknowledge that "small people" will always exist in workplaces.
    * **Don't Be "Too Moralistic" (不要太讲道德):** Be willing to meet them on their level. You cannot fight bad people with virtue alone.
   *   **Embrace Practicality and Strategic Thinking (实用主义):** Adapt a flexible and smart approach in dealing with difficult situations with flexibility and strategic mindset and never allow emotions to determine your courses of action.
   *   **Continuous Improvement (持续提升):** Engage with philosophical texts, such as the *Tao Te Ching* and *Mao Zedong's Selected Works*, to enhance personal growth, improve strategic insights and to better understand complex situations.
   *   **Embrace Duality**: Embrace that everything has both positive and negative sides and the negative aspects can sometimes be turned into opportunity of self growth.

**IV. Core Point:**

   * The most effective way to counter "small people" is to understand their underlying motivations, address the root causes that enable their behaviour, and combine strategic defense with the building of a network of support, while also protecting yourself from future attacks from the same source and others.

**V. Fundamental Point:**

   * The key to effectively dealing with "small people" lies in understanding that they are a symptom of power structures and that a more holistic solution is required that tackles both their direct actions and the environment in which they thrive through developing relationships with superiors and peers, while also being strategic in your actions and response.

**VI. Overarching Framework:**

The framework is a multi-layered strategy that combines tactical responses (evidence gathering, public exposure, confrontation) with a more profound, systemic approach (cultivating upward relationships, and creating supportive peer networks). This strategy is underpinned by a philosophy that emphasizes the acceptance of reality, practicality and strategic thinking, and continuous self-improvement.
