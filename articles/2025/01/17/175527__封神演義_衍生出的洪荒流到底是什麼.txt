**Summary:**

**I. Origin of the "Honghuang" (Primordial) Genre:**

*   **Foundation:**  Evolved from the classic novel *Investiture of the Gods* (*Fengshen Yanyi*).
*   **Impact:** Inspired countless online novels and authors, making it a highly profitable genre.
*   **Core Idea:** Unifies various Chinese myths into a cohesive, albeit often conspiratorial, narrative.

**II. Core Primordial Setting and Key Characters:**

*   **Creation:** Pangu's birth, his use of the Tai Chi diagram, and the creation of the primordial world (Honghuang).
*   **Major Deities:** Pangu's spirit becomes the Three Pure Ones (Sanqing), his blood the Twelve Ancestral Witches, and his eyes transform into Di Jun and Donghuang Taiyi.
*   **Conflicts:** Battles between good and evil, including the fight between Hongjun Laozu and Luo Hou Jidu, and later between the Witch and Demon races.

**III. The Events Leading to the Investiture of the Gods:**

*   **Establishment of Heaven:** Hongjun Laozu establishes the Thirty-Three Heavens, Donghuang Taiyi rules the Celestial Court, and Nuwa creates humans.
*   **Witch-Demon War:** The Witch and Demon races clash, leading to the collapse of Mount Buzhou, which prompts Nuwa's patching of the sky.
*   **The Sun-Shooting Incident:** Houyi shoots down nine of the ten sun crows (Di Jun's sons), leading to conflict.
*  **Fragmentation of Honghuang:** The Witch-Demon war shatters Honghuang into fragments, with the human world as the largest piece where the Three Teachings (Taoist, Chan, Jie) emerge.

**IV. The Investiture of the Gods (Fengshen) War:**

*   **Purpose:**  To appoint gods and fill positions in the Celestial Court.
*   **Hidden Agenda:** Yuan Shi Tianzun and Nuwa conspire to weaken the Jie Teaching.
*   **Key Figures:** Jiang Ziya is appointed to lead the investiture, and Shen Gongbao stirs up conflict.
*   **Outcome:**  The Jie Teaching is decimated, and the Three Pure Ones' brotherhood is broken, while Buddhism begins to rise.

**V. The Rise of Buddhism and Subsequent Conflicts:**

*   **Buddhism's Growth:** The Western Teachings (Buddhism) becomes a dominant force, with many shifting allegiance.
*   **Taoist Countermove:** Laozi goes west, transforming one of his disciples into the Buddha (Sakyamuni) in an attempt to split Buddhism.
*   **Monkey King (Sun Wukong):**  A key figure in Buddhist expansion, manipulated by Bodhi and then ultimately controlled by Buddha.
*   **Journey to the West:**  A significant event that further increased Buddhism's influence, orchestrated by Buddhism and the Heavenly Court.

**VI. Further Conflicts and Expansion:**

*  **Other Deities and Conflicts:** This includes conflicts like the story of Chen Xiang saving his mother and the story of White Snake and Xu Xian.
*   **The Cowherd and the Weaver Girl:** The story of the Cowherd and the Weaver Girl is used to further demonstrate the ongoing conflict between Buddhism and Taoism.
*   **Legacy:** The Honghuang genre integrates all Chinese myths, even if they are presented with a conspiratorial framework.

**Core Point:** The Honghuang genre, born from *Investiture of the Gods*, merges disparate Chinese myths into a single, complex narrative marked by continuous power struggles.

**Fundamental Point:** The "Honghuang" genre, while deeply rooted in mythology, functions as a narrative framework for exploring themes of power, betrayal, and religious conflict, with constant shifts in the balance of influence.

**Overarching Framework:** The overarching framework is a cyclical conflict for power and influence, where figures and factions, whether gods, demons, or humans, are constantly vying for dominance in a cosmos that's both vast and intricately connected.
