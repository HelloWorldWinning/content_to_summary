    *   The face reflects a person's inner state and past experiences.
    *   Facial balance (left and right) indicates inner balance.
    *   Uneven skin tone or texture can signify underlying issues.
    *   Features like wrinkles reveal life stages and hardships.
2.  **The Importance of "习" in Learning:**
    *   Traditional interpretation of learning as constant review is incorrect.
    *   Learning should lead to ingrained habits (习), not just memorization.
    *   True learning is actionable and becomes a natural part of behavior.
3.  **Cultivating Positive Relationships:**
    *   Building relationships requires offering value to others.
    *   True generosity and support are essential for lasting connections.
    *   Sincere actions should be for personal integrity and moral compass, not for recognition.
4.  **Avoiding Superficiality and Embracing Depth:**
    *   Avoid ostentatious displays of skill or knowledge.
    *   Focus on substance rather than superficial appearances.
    *   True mastery involves understated power and ethical conduct.
5.  **The Balance of Inner and Outer World:**
    *   One should not be easily provoked or affected by external forces.
    *   Self-transformation leads to positive influence on others.
    *   Focus on individual changes instead of changing the world.
6. **Organizational Dynamics and Leadership:**
    *   Understanding each role within the organization.
    *   High-level staff should focus on strategy, not micromanagement.
    *   Managers must nurture and delegate opportunities to others.
7.  **The Dangers of Unethical Actions:**
    *   Disregarding nature and ethical principles brings negative consequences.
    *   Focus on actions that are aligned with higher values rather than short term profits.
8.  **The Importance of Strategic Differentiation:**
    *   Avoid direct competition and focus on creating unique value.
    *   Develop a deep understanding of your role and focus on excelling within your position.
    *   Positioning and differentiation are important for lasting success.
9. **The Right Way to Reward and Encourage:**
    *   Personalize rewards to the recipient's specific needs and interests.
    *   Combine both tangible and intangible rewards for a balanced approach.
    *   Use encouragement and recognition for efforts, as well as for results.
10. **The importance of understanding and application**
    *   Understanding that any action done has a reason behind it, even if that reason might seem nonsensical at face value.
    *   The reasons may be valid at certain levels, and not others.
    *   That the best way to proceed with understanding and adapting oneself is by learning to listen, and not trying to force one's views and ideas onto another person.
