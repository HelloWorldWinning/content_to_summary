**Summary:**

**I. Core Idea: Identifying the "Watershed Point" (分水岭)**

  *   **A. Initial Stock Behavior:**
      *   The stock experiences a significant drop, then stabilizes, and then starts rising (下跌, 筑底, 拉升).
      *   Before the "Watershed Point," the stock will have patterns that make investors hesitant to buy or hold. This is manipulated by the main force (主力).
  *   **B. Characteristics of the "Watershed Point":**
       *   It is not an individual peak.
       *   It's identified by a major shift in the main force's control strategy.
       *   Prior to the point, the main force discourages investors to buy (让你不喜欢).
       *   After the point, the main force encourages investors to buy (让你喜欢).
       *   The change in strategy is reflected in the stock’s price action, e.g., from big gap downs and long upper wicks to more steady rises.
  *   **C. Identifying the Shift:**
       *   Compare the stock's behavior before and after; a change in how the stock price moves. For example:
           *   Before: Price fluctuations designed to make investors not want to hold.
           *   After: Price movements designed to make the investor feel comfortable buying.
       *   Look for the shift in how the stock opens, its daily volatility, and how it behaves.

**II. The Dangers of a "Watershed Point"**
    * **A. Post-Watershed Behavior:**
          * After the "Watershed Point," the stock will always or most likely drop significantly.
          * This drop may be direct and fast or gradual with a series of dips.
    * **B. Avoiding Losses:**
          * Do not buy after a "Watershed Point" has been identified.
          * It is very dangerous to average down if you have bought stock at a "Watershed Point".

**III. Trading Strategy**

*   **A. Recognizing the Pattern:** Be able to discern the change in stock control tactics at a "Watershed Point".
*  **B. Risk Management:** Avoid investing near or after the "Watershed Point" because it almost always leads to significant drops.

**Core Point:** The "Watershed Point" is a critical signal indicating a major shift in the stock's control by the main force and a high likelihood of a subsequent drop.

**Fundamental Point:**  Recognizing the subtle changes in stock price action reflecting a shift in the main force's manipulation tactics can help investors avoid potential losses.

**Overarching Framework:** The main force strategically controls stock prices, using different patterns to make investors feel differently. Understanding the "Watershed Point" which occurs when the main force switches from scaring investors off to encouraging buying is crucial for investment risk management.
