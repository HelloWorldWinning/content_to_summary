**Summary:**

1.  **NPD (Narcissistic Personality Disorder) in Marriage:**
    *   **Self-Centeredness:** Prioritizes their needs above all else; your needs are insignificant or dismissed.
    *   **Emotional Instability:**  Experiences extreme mood swings; reacts aggressively or with contempt when frustrated or not catered to.
    *   **Control:**  Attempts to control all aspects of your life, from your appearance to your social interactions and career.
    *   **Lack of Accountability:** Never takes responsibility for problems; always blames you for their negative behavior, using illogical justifications.
    *  **Emotional Manipulation:** Requires constant validation and emotional support but reciprocates little to none, even when you're in need. They view your emotional needs as burdens or opportunities for control rather than a chance to show true empathy.

2.  **Core Point:**  NPD individuals are fundamentally self-centered and manipulative within marriage, causing significant emotional harm to their partners.

3.  **Fundamental Point:** The core identifiers of NPD are lack of empathy and extreme self-centeredness; recognizing this is crucial for leaving an NPD relationship.

4.  **Overarching Framework:** The framework revolves around identifying key behavioral patterns of NPD individuals in marriage, emphasizing their self-centeredness, emotional instability, controlling tendencies, lack of accountability, and manipulative nature. Recognizing these patterns is critical for people trapped in relationships with NPD individuals, and helps highlight the profound emotional toll of being in this kind of situation.
