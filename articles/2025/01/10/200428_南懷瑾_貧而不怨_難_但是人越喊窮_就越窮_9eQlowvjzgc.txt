1. **评 (Píng - Criticism/Judgment) and 愿 (Yuàn - Resentment):**
    *   评 is not solely about economic status; it encompasses various forms, including knowledge-based assessments.
    *   A "knowledge-blind" person might perceive a knowledgeable one as rich.
    *  Knowledge itself is a form of wealth.
    *   愿 arises from people's dissatisfaction and can lead to complaints and conflicts.
    *   班评得到 (Bān Píng Dé Dào - accepting criticism) is a Chinese cultural principle, but difficult to achieve.
    *   The inability to accept criticism and lack of self-control are sometimes criticized as hindering Chinese progress.

2. **修养 (Xiūyǎng - Self-Cultivation):**
    *   班评得到 and 知足常证 (Zhī Zú Cháng Zhèng - contentment with one's lot) are personal cultivation goals, rarely achieved.
    *   It is a mistake to generalize the shortcomings of individuals to an entire culture. Chinese culture also has virtues like self-improvement.
    *   It's a self-test to not have arrogant behavior especially after success.
    *  Achieving inner peace and not being arrogant are more important than external relations.

3. **Parent-Child Relationships:**
    *   Differences in parental authority exist across generations and regions.
    *  Some parents maintain a traditional, authoritative demeanor; others are more like friends with their children.
    *   Maintaining parental dignity is not inherently wrong.
    *   Parents with the "I am the parent and you must obey" mindset may harbor entitlement.
    *   Pride/arrogance is difficult to avoid, but can be overcome through self-cultivation.

4. **愿 (Yuàn - Resentment) and its Causes:**
    *   愿 arises from hardship and the feeling of injustice, often directed towards external forces.
    *   People often blame heaven and others for their misfortune instead of themselves.
    *   This reaction is a way of escaping reality.
    *   怨天 (Yuàn Tiān - blaming heaven) is a common response in hardship.

5. **知足 (Zhī Zú - Contentment):**
    *   To avoid being discontent, one must practice contentment.
    *   Contentment is a form of achievement, leading to happiness.
    *   A discontent heart leads to more and more resentment.
    *  Knowing how to be content is a path to achieving true wealth and a happy life.
    *   True wealth is not measured by money but by happiness and the ability to help others.
    *  Contentment leads to a peaceful heart, and a peaceful heart leads to freedom and happiness.
    *  If you are content, you are always wealthy.

**Core Point:**

True wealth lies not in material possessions or external success but in cultivating inner peace, contentment, and the ability to help others.

**Fundamental Point:**

The key to personal well-being and a fulfilling life is to practice self-awareness, accept criticism, cultivate inner peace and contentment, and be truly willing to help others.

**Overarching Framework:**
