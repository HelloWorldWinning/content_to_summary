**I. Characteristics of NBD (Narcissistic Behavioral Disorder):**

*   **Extreme Self-Absorption:** NBD individuals are intensely self-centered and narcissistic, prioritizing their own needs and desires above all else.
*   **Lack of Remorse & Empathy:** They exhibit a complete absence of guilt, remorse, and empathy, demonstrating no ethical consideration for their actions.
*   **Self-Justification:** They possess a remarkable ability to rationalize their behavior, believing their actions are inherently justified, regardless of their negative impact on others.
*   **Cycle of Abuse:** NBD individuals engage in a repeating pattern of devaluing, discarding, and then manipulating others back into their orbit to maintain their inflated ego.
*   **Control & Manipulation:** They utilize their intelligence, which can be high, to control and manipulate those around them, focusing on gaining dominance and validation.
*   **Superficial Relationships:** They are interested in control and manipulation, not genuine connections, often avoiding clear boundaries in relationships to maintain power dynamics.

**II. Behavior Patterns of NBD:**

*   **Devaluation and Blame-Shifting:** NBDs consistently devalue and blame others, taking no personal responsibility for their actions.
*   **Victim Playing:** Despite being the perpetrators of harm, they often portray themselves as the victims to garner sympathy and avoid accountability.
*   **Initial Charm & Gradual Abuse:** They often initially present as charming and confident to lure people in, before slowly revealing their manipulative and abusive behaviors.
*   **Adaptability to Environment:** In harsh environments, they thrive due to their ruthless nature, while in developed societies, they become more subtle and manipulative (e.g., covert narcissists and/or antisocial personality disorder).

**III.  Recommendations for Dealing with NBD Individuals:**

*   **Sever Ties:**  Immediately end any relationship that involves devaluation, abuse, or cold treatment. Do not engage in self-blame.
*   **Beware of Perfect Facades:**  Recognize that a person's apparent success or perfection has nothing to do with their true character or morality.
*   **Avoid Naive Idealism:**  Do not project positive intentions onto others, and don't try to "save" them.
*   **Acknowledge Unchangeability:**  Accept that you cannot change a narcissistic personality, and avoid confrontation, which is futile.

**Core Point:** NBD individuals are characterized by a deep-seated lack of empathy, a relentless pursuit of self-validation, and a manipulative pattern of behavior aimed at exploiting others.
