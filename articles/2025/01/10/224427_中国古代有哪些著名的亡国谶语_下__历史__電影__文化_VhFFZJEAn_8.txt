**II. Specific Prophecies and Their Associated Events**

*   **A. "京道历任奇异之" (Jing Dao Li Ren Qi Yi Zhi):**
    *   This prophecy referred to the Southern Song dynasty's fate.
    *   It was interpreted to mean that the dynasty's lineage would be interrupted by someone with "Qi" in their name.
    *   General Xiao Daocheng, whose family name is "Xiao" which contains "Qi" as radical, usurped the throne, establishing the Qi dynasty.
*   **B. "陶离子，红水绕阳山" (Tao Li Zi, Hong Shui Rao Yang Shan):**
    *   This prophecy surfaced during the Sui Dynasty.
    *   The "Red Water" was interpreted as a threat to the Sui emperor's family.
    *   It led to suspicion and persecution of individuals with family names related to "Li" (which is part of "Tao Li Zi") or "Red", particularly the Li family.
    *   Li Yuan later used it to justify the establishment of Tang dynasty, claiming the name "Tao Li Zi" meant his lineage.
*   **C. "唐三世之后，女主武王代有天下" (Tang San Shi Zhi Hou, Nv Zhu Wu Wang Dai You Tian Xia):**
    *   This prophecy foretold that after three generations of the Tang Dynasty, a female monarch would take over.
    *   Emperor Taizong became wary and suspicious, leading him to execute a general with the name Li Junxian who had "Wu" and "Five" in his details, whom he believed fit the prophecy.
    *   Empress Wu Zetian eventually seized power, fulfilling the prophecy, which might have been created by herself.
*   **D. "点检做天子" (Dian Jian Zuo Tian Zi):**
    *   This prophecy arose during the Later Zhou Dynasty of the Five Dynasties period.
    *   It suggested that the palace commander would become the emperor.
    *   General Chai Rong removed the then commander Zhang Yongde, out of fear that Zhang would start a rebellion.
    *   Zhao Kuangyin, the new commander, later became the founding emperor of the Song Dynasty.
