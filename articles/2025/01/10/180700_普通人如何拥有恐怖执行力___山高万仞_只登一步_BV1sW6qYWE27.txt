**Summary:**

1.  **Problem with Traditional Approaches:**
    *   People tend to focus on distant, overarching goals ("遥远的山头") and the challenging path ahead ("起去的生物").
    *   This approach leads to feeling overwhelmed, tired, and discouraged before even starting.

2.  **Yang Ming's Philosophy:**
    *   Focus solely on the present moment ("眼前这一步").
    *   Break down large goals into manageable small steps.
    *   This mindset aligns with the Buddhist concept of "安住当下".

3.  **Key Distinction:**
    *   Long-term goals are important, but long-term *attention* is detrimental.
    *   Focusing on the distant future causes anxiety and exhaustion.
    *   It is crucial to differentiate between a long-term target and where your focus is at any given moment.

4. **Practical Application:**
    * Define clear goals but do not dwell on the end result.
    * Divide goals into smaller, reachable tasks.
    * Concentrate on completing the task at hand; not the entire project.
    * This focus on the present allows for consistent action and better performance.

**Core Point:** The key to achieving seemingly impossible goals is to focus solely on the present step rather than being overwhelmed by the grand objective.

**Fundamental Point:** Maintaining a long-term goal is important, but your primary attention must always remain on the present moment to avoid overwhelm and promote consistent progress.

**Overarching Framework:**
The content frames the challenge of achieving ambitious goals by contrasting two approaches:
1.  A problematic approach that emphasizes a big picture, distant perspective causing anxiety and exhaustion.
2.  An effective approach that champions present moment focus and manageable actions, leading to success.
