**Summary:**

**I. The Problem: University as a "High School" Repeat**
    *   **A. University Disconnect:**
        *   Universities are increasingly becoming like high schools, focusing on rote learning rather than critical thinking and self-discovery.
        *   Courses are often irrelevant and students learn without practical application.
    *   **B. Focus on "绩点" (GPA) & Internal Competition:**
        *   The primary goal becomes getting good grades, pursuing further studies (研究生/硕士), and obtaining certificates.
        *   Students are encouraged to compete with each other, leading to a lack of cooperation and a high-pressure environment.
    *   **C.  University's Priorities:**
        *   Universities prioritize research and funding over undergraduate education.
        *   Professors are often focused on research and publication, leading to less focus on undergraduate teaching quality.
        *   Teaching quality is often measured by surface metrics like attendance and exam pass rates rather than actual learning outcomes.
    *   **D.  Blind Pursuit of Academic Achievements:**
         * University creates a system where "优绩主义(meritocracy)"  is perceived as the only path to success.
         *  Students are conditioned to believe that high grades, scholarships, and graduate studies are the sole criteria for future success. This creates a system where students are pitted against each other, focusing on winning accolades instead of actual growth.
**II.  The Real World vs. University's Reality**
    *   **A.  Lack of Real-World Skills:**
        *   Students lack a proper understanding of the real world and are often unprepared for the job market.
        *   The university system prevents students from exploring their passions, as the focus is primarily on academics, exams, and activities.
    *  **B.  The Illusion of Meritocracy:**
        * Universities promote "merit-based" awards that are primarily for the sake of managing the student body and encouraging postgraduate education, not for the student's actual development.
    *  **C. Delaying Entry to the Workforce**
        * The educational system prolongs the time before young people can contribute to society, with many only entering the workforce in their late 20's.
    * **D. Obsession with Credentials rather than competency**
        *  Employers, especially in small and medium-sized enterprises, are increasingly disenchanted with graduates with good grades but without practical abilities.
    * **E.  The problem with credentials**
         *   Overemphasis on qualifications (certificates, awards) over actual skills creates a mindset of prioritizing outward appearances over genuine development.

**III.  What Should Students Do?**
    *   **A. Focus on "Hard Skills" (硬实力):**
        *   Students should focus on cultivating skills that are valuable in the real world, not just academic achievements.
        *   These hard skills are built through experience, work, and self-learning, not just formal education.
    *   **B.  Find your "Golden Currency"**:
        *  Do not be blind to the system, and instead invest your time to gain knowledge and skills that are recognized by the world, just like an economy should not be based on fiat currency without valuable assets.
    *   **C.  Discover Your "Ambition" (志向):**
        *   Explore interests and passions outside of the university curriculum.
        *   Don't fall into the trap of doing what everyone else is doing.
    *   **D. Don't be limited by the environment**:
         *  Do not see the school or your peers as the source of information, actively expand your horizons by reading books on different subjects, internships, and exploring.
    *  **E. Be active, instead of passive**:
         * Students need to take control of their own learning instead of passively waiting for the school to dictate their future.
    *  **F. Re-evaluate the University System**
         * Understand that the school system is often for the convenience of the administration, do not be overly occupied by the classes, meetings and activities.
        * Be aware of the suggestions from school faculty, and be critical of the reasoning behind it.
    *   **G.  Balance Academia and Personal Development:**
        *  Use university as a resource to self-study, and use other time to gain experience and explore interests.
         *  Actively reject low-efficiency activities, and take responsibility for your choices.
    *   **H.  Reflect on the Purpose of Education:**
        *   Understand that education should be about self-improvement, not just getting a job.

**Core Point:**  University systems often prioritize research and managing undergraduates over genuine student development, leading to an environment of internal competition and an obsession with qualifications rather than actual skills.

**Fundamental Point:** Students should focus on cultivating real-world "hard skills" and discovering their own ambitions rather than blindly following the university's system, which often prioritizes credentials over practical capabilities and knowledge.

**Overarching Framework:** The content is structured around a critique of the modern university system, highlighting its flaws and offering alternative perspectives for personal and professional development outside of traditional academia.
