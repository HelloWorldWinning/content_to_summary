2.  **Common Symptoms:**
    *   **Reduced Libido:** Lack of interest in sexual activity, decreased sexual fantasies, and distress after sex.
    *   **Erectile Dysfunction:** Less frequent nocturnal erections and difficulty maintaining erections during intercourse. Low testosterone can impact nitric oxide production, which is essential for erections.
    *   **Reduced Semen Volume:** Lower than 1.5 ml per ejaculation and less than 15 million sperm per ml of semen, leading to possible fertility issues. Testosterone and DHT stimulate the prostate and seminal vesicles to produce seminal fluid.
    *   **Sleep Disorders:** Difficulty falling asleep, short sleep duration, and frequent awakenings, although not unique to low testosterone.
    *   **Low Energy and Fatigue:** Poor sleep quality due to low testosterone can cause fatigue and lack of motivation, even without sleep issues.
    *   **Hot Flashes:** Episodes of heat and flushing, often accompanied by sweating, typically short-lived, common in women experiencing menopause, but possible in men with low testosterone levels.
3.  **Physical Changes:**
    *   **Decreased Muscle Mass:** Reduction in muscle mass and strength due to testosterone being an anabolic hormone essential for building and maintaining muscle.
    *   **Increased Body Fat:** Increase in overall body fat, particularly around the abdomen and back, leading to an unhealthy cycle of low testosterone and increased fat.
    *   **Bone Loss:** Increased risk of bone fractures due to low testosterone, as testosterone is essential for maintaining bone strength and density.
4.  **Mental and Emotional Effects:**
    *   **Depression and Anxiety:** Increased risk of depression, anxiety, and memory problems, as testosterone impacts brain function, mood, and cognition.
5.  **Other Symptoms:**
    *   **Anemia:**  Lack of sufficient red blood cells for oxygen transport due to low testosterone, which affects the production of red blood cells.
    *   **Hair Loss:** Androgenetic alopecia (male pattern baldness) and benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH) may accompany low testosterone.  DHT, a derivative of testosterone, has a role in controlling inflammation in hair follicles and the prostate.
    *   **Gynecomastia:** Development of female-like breast tissue due to an imbalance of testosterone and estrogen. Low testosterone can lead to a relative increase in estrogen levels.
6.  **Conclusion:**
    *   If men experience these symptoms, it's vital to seek medical consultation from a urologist or endocrinologist.
