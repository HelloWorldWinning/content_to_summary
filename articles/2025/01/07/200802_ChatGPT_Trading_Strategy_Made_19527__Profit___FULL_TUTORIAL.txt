**I. Introduction**

**II. Trading Strategy Components**
    * **A. Machine Learning K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN) Indicator:**
        * Analyzes historical market data to predict price movements.
        * KNN classifies price direction (up or down) based on nearest neighbors in feature space.
        * Uses historical price data transformed into feature vectors including technical indicators.
        * Provides buy/sell signals (blue/pink labels), but should not be used in isolation.
    * **B. Exponential Moving Average (EMA) Ribbon:**
        * Multiple EMAs stacked together to visualize trend direction and strength.
        * Upward sloping ribbon indicates an uptrend; downward indicates a downtrend.
        * Helps filter false signals but is not sufficient on its own.
    * **C. Relative Strength Index (RSI):**
       * Measures the strength of price action, ranging from 0-100.
        * Overbought (above 70, modified to 60); oversold (below 30, modified to 40).
        * The RSI is made more sensitive for more valid entries.

**III. Entry Conditions**
    * **A. Long Trade:**
        1. Price closes above the 200 EMA.
        2. EMA ribbon is above 200 EMA and is green.
        3. Price pulls back into the ribbon, without closing below the long-term EMA.
        4. Machine learning indicator prints a blue label.
        5. RSI is oversold before the buy signal
    * **B. Short Trade:** (Opposite of Long)
        1. Price and EMA ribbon below the 200 EMA, with the ribbon red.
        2. Price pulls back into the ribbon without closing above the 200 EMA.
        3. RSI is overbought during the pullback.
        4. Machine learning indicator gives a sell signal.
        5. RSI was not overbought at the time of the sell signal

**IV. Trade Management**
    * Set stop loss below recent swing low (long) or above recent swing high (short).
    * Target is twice the risk.
    * Move stop loss to break-even once a quarter of the profit is achieved.
    * Risk 5% of the account per trade.

**V. Backtesting and Results**
    * A $100 starting balance grew to $19,527 after 100 trades.
    * High risk per trade (5%) leads to higher drawdowns but also higher potential rewards.
    * Forward testing on a paper account is crucial before using real money.

**Core Point:** A high-risk trading strategy combining a machine learning indicator, EMA ribbon, and RSI with specific entry and management rules has demonstrated the potential for rapid account growth, albeit with a risk for high drawdowns.

**Fundamental Point:**  Combining multiple indicators and carefully managing risk are necessary for a successful high-risk trading strategy.
