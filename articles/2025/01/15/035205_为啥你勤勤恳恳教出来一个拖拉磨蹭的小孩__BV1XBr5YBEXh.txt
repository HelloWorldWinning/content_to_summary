**Summary:**

1.  **Problematic Learning Behaviors:**
    *   Child approaches learning with a "bartering" mentality (learning is a chore).
    *   Lacks initiative, relying on external prompting.
    *   Easily discouraged by difficulties, indicating a lack of effective learning strategies.

2.  **Ineffective Responses:**
    *   Pressuring, scolding, or punishing the child are counterproductive.
    *   Such methods exacerbate anxiety and resistance.

3.  **Effective Strategies:**
    *   **Reduce Criticism, Increase Guidance and Encouragement:**
        *   Capitalize on opportune moments (e.g., when the child is upset about exam results).
        *   Avoid constant nagging on small issues; choose battles wisely.
    *   **Provide Room for Experimentation:**
        *   Allow the child to make mistakes and learn from them.
        *   Focus on essential issues, not minor details.
    *   **Teach Effective Learning Methods:**
        *   Introduce the concept of "reverse learning" (逆习).
        *   Seek out resources to support efficient study habits.

4.  **Core Point:** The core point is that forcing, criticizing, and over-controlling a child's learning will not yield positive results; instead, focus on positive guidance, encouragement, and providing effective learning strategies.

5.  **Fundamental Point:** A child's negative learning behaviors often stem from a lack of positive learning experiences and effective methods; therefore, the key is to foster a supportive environment where they can explore, make mistakes, and learn from them.

6. **Overarching Framework:** The content frames the issues of a child's learning struggles in the context of parental responses, and provides solutions based on positive guidance, teaching new effective strategies, and providing opportunities for trial and error.
