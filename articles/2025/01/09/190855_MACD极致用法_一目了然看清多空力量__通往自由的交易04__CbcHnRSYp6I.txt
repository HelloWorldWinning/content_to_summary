**Summary:**

**I. Core Idea:**
    *   **Trend Following is Paramount:** The most important aspect of successful trading is to follow the trend of the market, prioritizing it over formations and structures.
    *   **Avoid Counter-Trend Trading:** Trading against the trend often leads to small profits followed by large losses because the market moves in the path of least resistance.
    *   **Simplify Complexity:** It is essential to simplify market analysis into a clear and visual form to understand variables, cycles, and patterns.
    *   **Dual MACD Indicator:** A dual MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) setup can visually clarify trend strength, volatility, trend position, and entry points.

**II. Trading Principles & Strategy:**
    *   **Market Orientation:**
        *   Use the 50-day and 100-day moving averages (or similar) on daily and weekly charts to define the market's direction.
        *   When moving averages trend downward and price is suppressed, focus on shorting; conversely, focus on longing when moving averages trend upward.
        *   This simple approach works because the market often has large single direction trends for extended periods.
    *   **Dual MACD Application:**
        *   Use a 55-89 period MACD (MACD #1) to define the trend's direction and strength. The histogram's height and depth indicate the intensity of the trend.
        *   Use a 13-21 period MACD (MACD #2) as an oscillating indicator.
        *   Look for divergence between the price and MACD #1 histogram to identify potential trend reversals.
        *   For entry signals, observe MACD #2: When it moves above (like a fish jumping out of the water) or below (like a submarine diving under water) the axis, look for opportunities to enter short or long positions, respectively.
        *   Also, you can chase the trend when MACD #2 keeps making deeper dives/jumps in the direction of the current trend.
    *  **Three-Level Analysis:**
        *   **Level 1: Big Picture (Trend):** The 55-89 MACD tells you whether you are in an up- or downtrend, like the deep sea water.
        *   **Level 2: Oscillations & Entries:** The 13-21 MACD helps you to enter at the most ideal time, like a fish in the sea.
        *  **Level 3: Structures (Big Picture) & Shapes (Microscope):** It simplifies the need for structural and pattern knowledge, focusing on trend following.
    * **Examples Provided**: The analysis with the Dual MACD method is applied to EURUSD, Gold, SPX, and BTC

**III. Core Point (Conclusion):**
    *   The most important rule in trading is to follow market trends and avoid counter-trend trades, always seeking to trade in the direction of the 'deep water.'

**IV. Fundamental Point (Conclusion):**
    *   Effective trading requires simplifying analysis into clear, visual indicators and tools that assist in trend following, as exemplified by the dual MACD strategy.

**V. Overarching Framework:**
    *   **Trend-Based Trading with Visual Simplification:** This content introduces a trading framework that emphasizes the importance of trend following and uses a visual indicator, the Dual MACD, to achieve this. This framework prioritizes ease of use and direct visual cues to aid in decision-making, removing unnecessary complexities found in other trading methods.
