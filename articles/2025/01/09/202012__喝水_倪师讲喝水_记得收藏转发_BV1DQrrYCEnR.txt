### Summary

**1. 503 Syndrome (五苓散证) Overview**

*   **Cause:** Imbalance of water distribution in the body where water accumulates on the skin and muscles (外), but is lacking in the intermediate tissues (中焦). This leads to thirst, and water retention, and thus, frequent vomiting after drinking water.
*   **Manifestations:**
    *   Thirst with vomiting after drinking.
    *   Impaired urination (小便不利).
    *   Edema (全身水肿).
    *   Sometimes fever (退热退烧).

**2. Herbal Actions and Mechanisms**

*   **Ze Xie (泽泻):**
    *   Directs water from the whole body to the abdomen.
    *   Concentrates water in the middle burner.
*   **Fu Ling (茯苓):**
    *   Facilitates water circulation in the three burners, particularly the middle burner (中焦).
    *   Strengthens the spleen (健脾).
    *   Used for fluid retention due to its water-regulating properties.
*   **Zhu Ling (猪苓):**
    *   Directs water towards the lower burner (下焦).
    *   Promotes urination.
*   **Gui Zhi (桂枝):**
    *   Strengthens the heart and muscles (强心肌).
    *   Accelerates blood circulation.
    *   Its strengthening effect is different from Rou Gui (肉桂).
*   **Bai Zhu (白术):**
    *   Strengthens the spleen (健脾) and removes dampness (祛湿).
    *   Improves focus and attention when the spleen is weak.
    *   Works together with Fu Ling.
    *   Used when the spleen doesn't want to be normal.

**3. Therapeutic Application**

*   **Combined Action:** Ze Xie, Fu Ling, and Zhu Ling redistribute water, while Gui Zhi and Bai Zhu strengthen the body and resolve water retention.
*   **Pediatric Use:**  503 can quickly alleviate edema in children because their bodies are simpler and more responsive to treatments.
*   **Administration:** For children, powder is recommended over decoctions (汤剂).
*   **Application:**
    *   Used for water retention in the body.
    *   Also, can be used to treat fever and general fluid imbalance.

**4. Identifying Cold Patterns**

*   **Pulse Diagnosis:**
    *   Cold patterns can be identified by observing the pulse.
    *   A slow pulse, where one breath corresponds to one pulse cycle, suggests a cold condition, compared to two pulses with two breaths in normal situations.
    *   This indicates a lack of heart warmth.

**Core Point:** The 503 syndrome is characterized by water accumulation on the surface and lack in the middle, leading to thirst, vomiting after drinking, and fluid retention, addressed through specific herbal actions to redistribute water and strengthen the body.

**Fundamental Point:** Understanding the interplay of different herbs in 503, such as Ze Xie for water redirection, Fu Ling for middle burner circulation, and Gui Zhi and Bai Zhu for strengthening, is crucial for effective therapeutic interventions in cases of fluid imbalances.

**Overarching Framework:** The content outlines the 503 syndrome in Traditional Chinese Medicine, detailing its manifestations, the roles of individual herbs in addressing this syndrome, and diagnostic techniques for identifying underlying cold patterns and the mechanism.
