1.  **NPD (Narcissistic Personality Disorder) in Marriage: Core Issues**

    *   **Self-Centeredness:** NPD individuals prioritize their own needs and desires, disregarding their partner's.
    *   **Emotional Instability:** They exhibit extreme mood swings, often triggered by unmet needs or perceived slights, leading to anger and volatile behavior.
    *   **Controlling Behavior:** They seek control over various aspects of their partner's life, including appearance, social interactions, and career.
    *   **Lack of Accountability:** They never take responsibility for problems or conflicts, consistently blaming their partner.
    *   **Exploitation and Lack of Empathy:** They require constant emotional validation from their partner without reciprocating, often exploiting their partner's vulnerabilities. They show no empathy and provide no support when their partner is suffering.

2.  **Core Point:** NPD individuals in marriage are fundamentally self-centered, emotionally unstable, and controlling, leading to a toxic relationship for their partner.

3.  **Fundamental Point:** Identifying an NPD partner's core traits – lack of empathy and extreme self-centeredness – is crucial for victims to take steps to protect themselves.

4.  **Overarching Framework:** The content presents a framework for understanding how Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD) manifests in marital relationships, highlighting the specific behaviors and their impact on the non-NPD partner.
