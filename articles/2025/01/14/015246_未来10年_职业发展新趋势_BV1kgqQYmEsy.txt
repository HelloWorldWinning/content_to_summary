        1.  **1990s:** Transition from planned to market economy; laid-off state-owned enterprise workers (国企下岗).
        2.  **2008 Financial Crisis:** Collapse of export and foreign trade bubbles; layoffs.
        3.  **2012 E-commerce Boom and Bust:** Collapse of many group-buying platforms; job losses.
        4.  **2016 O2O Boom and Bust:** Similar industry bubble and job loss.
        5.  **2019 Internet Finance Bust:** Another industry downturn.
        6. **2021 Double Reduction Policy Impact:** Education and tutoring sector upheaval.
        7.  Each wave caused large-scale unemployment, affecting millions.
   * **C. Patterns of Job Market Fluctuations**
        1.  The cycle between transformations is shortening, increasing uncertainty for younger generations.
        2.  Rapidly developing sectors attract significant capital, leading to inflated salaries and rapid hiring, followed by inevitable collapses and job losses.

   * **D. Three Major Trend-Driven Changes**
        1.  **New Technology:**
             *   New energy vehicle replacement.
             *   AI's impact across all industries (replacing traditional jobs and creating new opportunities).
        2.  **New Consumer Groups:**
            *  Emergence of international markets
            *   Aging population's new needs;
            *  Changing consumer preferences due to shrinking family size, delayed marriage.
         3.  **New Media Channels:**
            *   Short-video and live-streaming changing communication and trust-building;
            * Self-media's influence outstripping traditional media; creating new professions.

   * **E. Three Future Career Trends**
        1.  **Multiple Income Streams (Slashie Lifestyle):** Combining main and side hustles.
        2.  **Interdisciplinary Skills:** The ability to integrate different skill sets, utilize new media, and execute ideas.
        3.  **Devaluation of Academic Credentials:** Increasingly less aligned with actual market demand.
  * **F. The Changing Nature of Work**
        1.  Lower barriers to entrepreneurship due to self-media and AI.
        2.  Shift from traditional centralized structures to more fluid, networked organizations.
        3. The importance of executing entire business processes; not just one skill.

   * **G. The Evolution of Job Roles**
        1.  **Key/Pillar Roles (支点性岗位):** R&D, high barriers to entry.
        2.  **Gain-Generating Roles (刚感性岗位):** Sales, marketing, etc.; facing challenges from "super individuals".
        3. **Capacity/Production Roles (产能性岗位):** Manufacturing, design; vulnerable to AI.
        4. **Lubricating/Administrative Roles (润滑性岗位):** Admin, HR, etc.; shrinking due to AI and streamlined businesses.

   * **H. Skills for the Future**
        1. The future belongs to those who can combine the four role abilities above; design, sell, improve, and outsource.
        2.   The need to create product or service systems to generate passive income; labor is easily replaced.

   * **I. Income Models**
         1. Labor-based: Selling time for money; vulnerable and low ROI.
        2. Product/Service-based: Directly selling to market; more autonomy and higher earning.
        3. System-based: Leveraging systems to scale revenue; the key to wealth and independence.

   * **J. Conclusion: Building Your Own System**
        1.  Leverage scalable systems (media, products) over manual labor.
        2.  Focus on identifying and solving societal problems, which translates to new market opportunities and wealth.
