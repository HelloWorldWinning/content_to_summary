### Summary Outline:

**I. Introduction**

*   The speaker is Yu Xin from Hanzi Technology, focusing on their product, MOMEN, for overseas markets.
*   MOMEN is a no-code development platform for websites and applications, similar to their domestic product, Zion.
*   Key differences between Zion and MOMEN include: Zion supports WeChat mini-programs, while MOMEN has an AI-powered feature called "AI Agent Border".
*   Target audience: Non-technical entrepreneurs ("None Technique Funner") who have business ideas but lack the tools to implement them.
*   Future plan: Support mobile app development.

**II. Timeline and Initial Progress**

*   Overseas launch: May 2023, after three years of domestic operation.
*   Adjustments: Differences in payment gateways (Stripe vs. WeChat/Alipay), and data grids
*   Early successes: Low customer acquisition cost ($1 per registered user), wide reach (over 100 countries due to content impact), and Portal Hunt recognition.
*   Achieved Product-Market Fit (PMF) by October 2024.

**III. Key Stages in Product Launch & Finding PMF**

*   **Product Release:**
    *   Avoid common mistakes: Don't expect immediate popularity; a functional website, tutorials, and user cases are crucial; early paid advertising should be avoided due to unstable product fit.
*   **Conversion Path:**
    *   Focus on conversion, not just acquisition.
    *   Six-step conversion path: Awareness, Trust, Registration, Usage, Payment, and Sharing.
    *   Building Trust is essential:
        *   Provide user cases for inspiration.
        *   Show relevant security certifications (SOC2, ISO27001).
        *   Display awards and press coverage for credibility.
        *   Show product updates and bug fixes to ensure sustainability.
*   **Finding PMF:**
    *   It's an iterative process, moving from assumptions to verification.
    *   Where to find users:
        *   Look at competitor users, and target pain points competitors don't address.
        *   Search for users in relevant user scenarios.
*  **User Acquisition Methods:**
     *   Direct Message Outreach
    *   Content Marketing on Reddit
    *   Competitor Analysis Tool
    *   SEO-driven Traffic

**IV. Maintaining Contact with Users & Achieving PMF**

*   Continuous feedback from users is essential, with priority to long-term users/paying customers.
*   Multiple ways to communicate:
     *   Monitor user behavior via tools.
    *   Establish feedback channels (product internal & external).
    *   Organize regular user forums.
*   Achieving PMF (October 2024):
    *   Evidence includes repeated users and payment, user case stories, SOPO users and some word of mouth promotion
*   Ongoing Activities after PMF:
    *   Continuous Exploration of Market Demand
    *   Content Creation: Showcase and Tutorial
    *   Partnerships with educational institutions and companies

**V. Key Insights & Takeaways**

*   The importance of building a team with AI automation capabilities.
    *   Utilize AI to automate repetitive tasks, allowing teams to focus on valuable tasks.
    *   AI-powered tools used: Automation, Operations, analysis and web development.
*   Founders must be involved in early-stage communication with users.
*   Portal Hunt should be strategically used for brand building.
*   Content marketing and SEO are vital for long-term growth:
    *   A 3-layered content strategy: informational, comparison, tutorial content is proposed.
*   Offer to AIGZ Gank users with 100 USD credits.

**Core Point:** The journey to product-market fit (PMF) requires patience, continuous adaptation, and a strong focus on understanding and addressing user needs.

**Fundamental Point:** Prioritizing user feedback and strategic automation are critical in achieving product-market fit, especially in the initial stages of launching a product overseas.

**Overarching Framework:** The framework includes a journey of initial product release and adjustments, iteratively building a conversion path focusing on conversion rather than simply customer acquisition, actively engaging with users for their feedback in a variety of ways, and finally obtaining PMF.
