**Summary Outline:**

1.  **Current Economic Paradox:**
    *   The current economy is characterized by a simultaneous presence of deflation and stagflation, not purely one or the other.
    *   This mixed state results in a differentiated impact across income levels.

2.  **Impact on Different Income Levels:**
    *   **Upper Class:**  Experiences deflation in asset prices but maintains stable incomes.
    *   **Middle Class:** Sees minimal income reduction but faces asset devaluation and indifference to rising consumer prices.
    *   **Lower Class:**  Struggles with job insecurity, stagnant/reduced income, and heightened living costs for essential goods.
    *   This creates a dual pressure on the lower class, leading to a perception of overall economic stagnation.

3.  **Data Analysis:**
    *   CPI rose by 0.3% in October, indicating a general price increase.
    *   Raw material costs surged by 21.6%, and food/alcohol prices went up by 0.5-0.6%.
    *   Meanwhile, major consumer goods and second-hand housing prices dropped by 5.3% and 6.89% respectively.
    *   This data underscores the co-existence of inflation and deflation – a ‘debt-driven stagflation’.

4.  **Underlying Mechanisms:**
    *   **Debt-Driven Stagflation:** Core issues are high debt ratios and long-term under-consumption.
    *   **Supply vs Demand Imbalance:**  Production consistently outstrips consumption, leading to deflationary pressures.
    *   **Wealth Distribution:** Past policies like land-based finance exacerbated wealth inequality, favouring the upper class and early entrants into the market.

5.  **Consequences of Existing Structure:**
    *   **Stagnant Domestic Demand:**  Wealthy individuals prefer wealth preservation over spending, and heavily indebted individuals struggle to increase consumption.
    *   **Limits to Traditional Stimulus:** In such a structural environment, traditional economic stimulus measures prove largely ineffective.

6.  **Solutions & Recommendations:**
    *   **Structural Reforms:** The key to resolving the situation is to initiate structural reforms, especially in labor income distribution.
    *   **Industry Upgrade:**  Focus on industrial upgrading to address bottlenecks.
    *   **Income Redistribution:**  Adjust income allocation to lower the cost of living for the lower strata or to actively increase lower strata income.

7.  **Strategic Direction:**
    *  The current development direction, emphasizing innovation and digitalization, despite setbacks, will continue towards a more prosperous future.

**Core Point:** The current economy is in a state of "debt-driven stagflation," characterized by the coexistence of inflation in essential goods and deflation in assets, largely driven by structural imbalances and income inequality.

**Fundamental Point:** To break the cycle of debt-driven stagflation, structural reforms focused on labor income redistribution and industry upgrading are crucial to restore balance and stimulate domestic demand.

**Overarching Framework:** The overarching framework is a macro-economic analysis of the current state, identifying the duality of stagflation and deflation, explaining its underlying causes in terms of debt, income disparity, and supply-demand imbalance. It then proposes structural reforms as the solution.
