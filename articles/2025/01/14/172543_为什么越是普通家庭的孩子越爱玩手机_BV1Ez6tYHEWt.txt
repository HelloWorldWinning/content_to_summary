    *   **Alternatives' High Cost:** Traditional activities like amusement parks and high-quality toys are economically infeasible.
    *   **"Value Proposition" of Mobile Phone:** Despite its potential downsides, mobile phones are seen as the most cost-effective way to occupy children.

    *   **Economic Divide:** The economic disparity significantly impacts the choices families can make, leading to differing developmental experiences.

    *   **Consequences:** Perceptions of injustice and resentment can arise.

**Core Point:** The prevalence of mobile phone use among children from lower-income families is largely driven by economic factors and the high cost of alternative forms of entertainment, making it a value-for-money choice despite drawbacks.

**Fundamental Point:** The real challenge lies in the intersection of poverty, limited access to resources, and a system that makes cheap digital entertainment the most viable option for many families.
