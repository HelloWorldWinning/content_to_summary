## Summary of the Content:

**1. Core Point:** The core issue isn't parental lack of resources, but rather parents' self-centeredness, narrow-mindedness, and the imposition of their own limited views on their children, causing resentment and hindering the child's growth.

**2. Overarching Framework:** The content explores the complex and often painful dynamic between children and their parents in Chinese families. It argues that children's resentment stems from parents' emotional immaturity, their tendency to project their own insecurities and frustrations onto their children, and their demand for obedience, which stifles the child's individuality. The article also touches on the child's feelings of guilt and responsibility for their parents' happiness, and the importance of breaking free from this cycle by prioritizing self-care and individual growth.

**3. Detailed Outline:**

*   **The Source of Resentment:**
    *   Not lack of resources, but parents' negative traits:
        *   Selfishness, vanity, being know-it-all.
        *   Narrow-mindedness and imposing their views.
        *   Inability to solve problems and blaming the family.
    *   This behavior traps the child, leading to feelings of hopelessness.
*   **Breaking Free:**
    *   Independent living is crucial.
    *   Cultivate independence and self-awareness.
    *   Trying to reconcile with parents might be futile.
*   **The Nature of Parental Love in China:**
    *   "Just enough" love, balancing joy and resentment.
    *   Guilt and pain intertwined with love.
    *   Conflicting messages (saving money vs. asking for help).
*   **Parents' Projection of Suffering:**
    *   Transferring pain by complaining about their hardships.
    *   Guilt-tripping children who want to enjoy life.
    *   Blaming children for marital or other problems.
    *   This creates guilt and a feeling of responsibility in the child.
*   **The Problem with Obedience:**
    *   The paradox of "listening" vs. personal happiness.
    *   Parents desire obedience for security and control.
    *   This robs children of their individuality and independence.
    *   Healthy individuals are not blindly obedient.
*   **The Solution: Self-Care for Parents:**
    *   Prioritize self-care as parents.
    *   Avoid being overly critical of oneself.
    *   Accept imperfections and allow mistakes.
