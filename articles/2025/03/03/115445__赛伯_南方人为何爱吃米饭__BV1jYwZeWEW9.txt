**II.  Southern Diet & Health:**

*   **A. Climate & "杖气" (Zhang Qi - Heat-Humidity Ailments):**
    *   Southern China experiences prolonged periods of high heat and humidity, leading to "杖气."
*   **B. Rice and Tea as Countermeasures:**
    *   Rice consumption (especially rice porridge) provides "干凉精之气" (dry, cooling essence) to counteract heat.
    *   Drinking tea complements this by further reducing heat.
*   **C. Digestive Issues & Heat Imbalance:**
    *   High heat leads to "气积寻还不畅" (Qi stagnation) and digestive issues.
    *   Consumption of rich, greasy foods exacerbates these problems, leading to symptoms like sore throat, mouth ulcers, and acne ("上火").
    *   Summer heat weakens digestive functions ("皮胃扬气空虚"), reducing appetite.

**III. Northern Diet & Health:**

*   **A. Climate & Diet:**
    *   Significant temperature variations throughout the year.
    *   The digestive system is relatively strong.
*   **B. Dietary Needs:**
    *   Northerners can generally tolerate richer foods without "上火" (heat-related symptoms).
    *   Rice porridge still provides necessary nourishment, especially in winter, but it is less essential than in the south.

**IV. Core Point:**

Southern Chinese rely on rice and tea to counteract the effects of heat and humidity, while northerners, with a more temperate climate, have different dietary needs and a stronger digestive system.
