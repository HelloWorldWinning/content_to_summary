**1. Eye Health and Vision:**

*   **Core Principle:** Vision issues stem from imbalances of blood (阴) and Qi (阳).
*   **Blood (阴) & Qi (阳):**
    *   *Blood:* Related to the left eye, heavy, horizontal, and connected to the heart via water. Governed by 清水 (clear water) which protects heart.
    *   *Qi:* Related to the right eye, rises upwards, and is responsible for movement and form.
*   **Vision Problems:**
    *   *Imbalance:* Causes blurred or distorted vision.
    *   *Blood imbalance*: cause shaking visions
    *   *Slanted vision:* May indicate imbalance of water and Qi.

**2. Approaches to Learning Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM):**

*   **Two Main Approaches:**
    *   *Method 1: Gradual Introduction:* Starting with basic principles (笔划 医进, 废波雄批), progressing to core TCM concepts, and then delving into classical texts like *Huangdi Neijing (黄帝内经)* and *Shen Nong Ben Cao Jing (神农本草经)*. This method requires more time and commitment.
    *   *Method 2: Holistic Overview:* Starting with a comprehensive overview of TCM, understanding the central concepts, and then studying classical texts. This approach helps to prevent being misled by the literal interpretations of the texts.
*   **Importance of Context:** Understanding the thought process of classical TCM practitioners like Zhang Zhongjing requires understanding the foundation he built upon, namely *Huangdi Neijing (黄帝内经)* and *Shen Nong Ben Cao Jing (神农本草经)*.
