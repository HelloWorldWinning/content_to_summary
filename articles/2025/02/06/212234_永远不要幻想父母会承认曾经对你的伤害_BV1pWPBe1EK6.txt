**Outline:**

1.  **The Problem:** Parents often struggle to admit their mistakes in parenting, even when their actions hurt their children.
2.  **Children's Perspective:** Children often seek acknowledgment of past hurts, not necessarily apologies, hoping for validation of their experiences.
3.  **Parents' Reactions:** Parents may deny, deflect, or minimize their actions, causing further pain.
4.  **Psychological Explanation:** Parents avoid admitting fault due to societal expectations of parental authority and a desire to avoid guilt.
5.  **Consequences for Children:** Children internalize blame, leading to self-doubt, insecurity, and an inability to address problems effectively.
6.  **Path to Healing:**
    *   Recognize parents' limitations and practice课题分离 (separating one's own issues from those of one's parents).
    *   Learn to attribute causes correctly, avoiding self-blame and recognizing lack of experience.
    *   Practice self-compassion and acknowledge personal progress, even if it's slower than others'.

**Fundamental Point:** By understanding that parents are imperfect and separating one's own issues from theirs, individuals can begin to heal and build a healthier sense of self.

**Overarching Framework:** The content describes the challenges arising from unacknowledged parental mistakes, the resulting self-blame in children, and strategies for personal healing through recognizing parental limitations, practicing accurate attribution, and fostering self-compassion.
