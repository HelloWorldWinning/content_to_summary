**Outline Summary:**

1.  **The Problem:**
    *   Many parents struggle to acknowledge their mistakes in raising their children, even when those mistakes cause harm.
    *   Children often seek acknowledgment of past hurts, not necessarily apologies, but parents frequently react defensively, denying or minimizing the impact.
    *   This denial deepens the child's wounds and leads to self-blame and insecurity.
2.  **Why Parents Deny:**
    *   Traditional beliefs position parents as infallible.
    *   Parents rationalize their actions to alleviate guilt, often using justifications like "I did it for your own good."
3.  **Consequences for the Child:**
    *   Children internalize the blame, leading to feelings of inadequacy, self-doubt, and avoidance of challenges.
    *   This results in low self-esteem, sensitivity, and a fear of facing reality.
4.  **The Solution: Self-Healing:**
    *   **Recognize Parental Limitations:** Accept that parents are imperfect individuals with their own biases and limitations.
    *   **Practice Issue Separation:** Distinguish between what is the parent's responsibility and what is your own.
    *   **Correct Attribution:** Avoid blaming yourself for everything; recognize external factors and lack of experience as potential causes.
    *   **Self-Affirmation:** Practice self-compassion and positive self-talk.
5.  **Encouragement:**
    *   Healing from the effects of a difficult childhood takes time.
    *   Acknowledge your progress and be patient with yourself.
    *   You are already doing great by addressing your inner struggles.

**Core Point:** Recognizing parental limitations, practicing issue separation, and cultivating self-compassion are key to overcoming the negative impacts of a difficult upbringing.

**Fundamental Point:** Self-healing from childhood wounds requires understanding that parental fallibility exists, coupled with the acceptance of self-responsibility and the practice of self-affirmation.

**Overarching Framework:** The content presents a problem (the impact of parents' unwillingness to acknowledge mistakes), explores the reasons behind the problem, examines the consequences for the children, and then provides a solution based on self-awareness, issue separation, and self-compassion.
