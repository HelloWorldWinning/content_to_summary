**Core Point:** The traditional path of education, from kindergarten to university, often leads to unemployment or low-paying jobs, primarily benefiting educators, educational institutions, and the housing market (specifically, "学区房") while leaving graduates burdened with debt and limited career prospects.

**Overarching Framework:** The content presents a critical perspective on the value of higher education in the current job market. It argues that the immense investment in education doesn't guarantee financial success and primarily benefits other sectors like education and real estate. It also suggests that delaying entry into the workforce through further education (like postgraduate studies) is a mechanism to delay the realization of unemployment and continue contributing to GDP without personal financial gain. The analysis draws a parallel between investing in education and getting married/having children, both seen as contributing to the system without necessarily leading to individual prosperity.
