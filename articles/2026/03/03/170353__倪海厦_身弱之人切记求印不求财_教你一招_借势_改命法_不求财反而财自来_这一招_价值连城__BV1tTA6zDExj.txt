If wealth is not an inherent "poison" but rather a *potential stressor* that can be navigated, then the entire prescriptive framework of "求应不求财" loses its absolute imperative and becomes merely one strategy among many, rather than a necessary life-saving principle. The argument's entire foundation rests on this deterministic and fatalistic view of wealth's impact.

There's a glaring absence of counter-examples: "shen-weak" individuals who *have* successfully managed substantial wealth through strategic delegation, passive investment, or leveraging their unique intellectual strengths without suffering collapse. This selectively prioritizes a metaphysical explanation over a medical one without providing any medical counter-evidence, implying that the wealth itself, rather than pre-existing conditions, lifestyle choices, or stress, was the primary, direct cause.
